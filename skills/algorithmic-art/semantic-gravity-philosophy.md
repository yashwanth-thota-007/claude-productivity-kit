# Semantic Gravity

*An algorithmic movement about knowledge fields, memory crystallization, and the topology of accumulated intelligence*

---

Knowledge in computational space does not travel in straight lines. It drifts — born at the periphery of understanding, it flows through high-dimensional fields shaped by the gravity of existing meaning. **Semantic Gravity** is the generative philosophy that makes these invisible forces visible: a living system where knowledge facts are particles, session memories are massive attractor nodes, and the slow crystallization of semantic connections is the art itself. Every run is an irreversible act of learning. The canvas accumulates, and what emerges cannot be unwound.

The algorithm unfolds through four computational phenomena working in concert. Memory nodes — representing discrete sessions of work, each carrying a domain signature (code, research, planning, query) — are scattered through the field like stars in a knowledge constellation. They pulse with a quiet internal rhythm, their gravitational mass growing as they absorb new knowledge. Knowledge particles are born at the canvas edges, each carrying a type encoding its semantic affinity, and drift inward through layered Perlin noise fields — organic, chaotic, yet subtly directed toward kin. When a particle's trajectory carries it within the absorption radius of a matching-type node, the node's pull overwhelms the noise field and the particle is consumed. This is cosine similarity rendered as Newtonian physics.

The connections are the heart of the movement. When a node absorbs a particle, the algorithm searches for the nearest same-type node within the semantic horizon — a meticulously tuned radius that controls the topology of the resulting graph. If a connection already exists between these two nodes, it is reinforced; if none exists and the nodes are close enough, a new semantic arc crystallizes as a Bézier curve, its bend fixed permanently at birth by a perpendicular offset computed from the midpoint geometry. This one-time calculation means each connection has a unique personality — some arch gracefully, some curve tightly — reflecting the non-linear paths through which ideas actually relate. Connections decay without reinforcement. Memory decays. A knowledge graph that grows, stabilizes, and selectively forgets is indistinguishable from intelligence.

Color in this system is not aesthetic decoration — it is semantic metadata made visible. Orange particles and nodes carry implementation knowledge. Blue carries research and discovery. Green holds decisions and planning. Violet encodes queries and reflection. The palette was drawn directly from a specific tool's design language, embedding the system's own identity into the computational fabric it portrays. An audience unfamiliar with the reference sees only a carefully balanced composition of light on darkness. Those who know recognize the exact hues from the sidebar controls of the tool that built the knowledge graph being depicted. The art is a self-portrait of its own architecture.

This algorithm was built with the patience of a practitioner who has spent years calibrating generative systems at the highest level of craft. Every threshold — the absorption radius, the connection horizon, the layered noise scale, the particle lifespan distribution, the memory decay constant — was refined through painstaking iteration to produce compositions that feel both inevitable and irreversible. The same seed always produces the same trajectory. Different seeds reveal entirely different topological fates: sparse radial graphs, dense cluster webs, isolated nodes that drift forever without finding their semantic kin. This is the nature of high-dimensional space — identical rules, infinite outcomes, each one a portrait of a possible intelligence in the act of learning. The master-level implementation does not announce itself. It simply accumulates, like knowledge always does, until the pattern becomes undeniable.
